Routes provide athletes with ways to plan and share their rides and runs with others.

Once a route has been created, it can be exported to a GPS device, printed as a cue sheet, and most importantly, shared with others, inside and outside of Strava.

The goal for the design of routes was to create something clean and simple that could be built upon over time. The actual route page has a flexible hierarchy that will allow for future expansion and features like photos, stories, ratings, and comments.

Route pages are made available to logged-out users in a basic form - showing just the map with a route path. If a logged-out user attempts to save or export a route page, they are prompted to sign up for Strava to gain full access to the routes feature.
